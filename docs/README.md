# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

Welcome to the OctoAcme project management documentation hub. This folder contains the process guides, templates, and reference materials that define how OctoAcme plans, executes, and continuously improves its software delivery.

## Overview

OctoAcme uses a lightweight but structured project management approach that guides work from initiation through planning, execution, release, and retrospective improvement. Projects begin with a clear business need, measurable goals, stakeholder alignment, and an initial risk and resource view captured in a one-pager. Once approved, work moves into planning, where teams break scope into shippable increments, define acceptance criteria, estimate effort, document dependencies, and agree on milestones, release plans, and a definition of done. This lifecycle is designed to keep delivery iterative, visible, and tied to outcomes rather than just activity.

Roles are clearly defined to support shared ownership and smooth delivery. Project Managers coordinate execution, timelines, risk management, and stakeholder communication, while Product Managers define desired outcomes, prioritize the backlog, and measure success. Developers are responsible for implementation, testing, technical collaboration, and surfacing technical risks, and QA contributors validate feature quality and acceptance criteria. Stakeholders provide input, approvals, and alignment throughout the project. Together, these roles create a cross-functional model where accountability is explicit but collaboration remains central.

Communication is handled through regular cadences and clear escalation paths. OctoAcme emphasizes weekly PM and product syncs, delivery-team standups, milestone or sprint demos, and recurring stakeholder updates. Teams maintain a single source of truth through project documentation, boards, and release materials, and they use structured templates for status updates, risk communication, and incident messaging. Risks and blockers are reviewed continuously, with escalation moving from team triage to PM and product leadership, and then to sponsor-level attention when business impact is significant.

Quality assurance is built into delivery rather than treated as a final checkpoint. Execution practices call for small pull requests, linked issues, documented acceptance criteria, and CI-based linting, automated tests, and security scanning before review or merge. Teams are expected to use unit tests for new logic, integration tests where needed, and end-to-end smoke tests for critical flows before release, alongside manual QA for acceptance when appropriate. Release readiness also includes rollback planning, release notes, staging validation, production verification, and post-release communication. After milestones, sprints, or incidents, retrospectives capture lessons learned and convert them into tracked action items so the process keeps improving over time.

## How to Use

**New to OctoAcme?** Start with the [Project Management Overview](octoacme-project-management-overview.md) to understand our core principles, roles, and delivery lifecycle, then review [Roles and Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md) to understand where you fit.

**Starting a new project?** Follow [Project Initiation](octoacme-project-initiation.md) to prepare your project charter and stakeholder alignment, then move to [Project Planning](octoacme-project-planning.md) to build your backlog and release plan.

**Actively delivering?** Use [Execution and Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md) as your day-to-day reference and [Risks and Communication](octoacme-risks-and-communication.md) to manage blockers and keep stakeholders informed.

**Approaching a release?** Follow the [Release and Deployment](octoacme-release-and-deployment.md) guide to complete readiness checks, coordinate deployment, and communicate the release.

**Wrapping up a milestone or sprint?** Use [Retrospective and Continuous Improvement](octoacme-retrospective-and-continuous-improvement.md) to capture learnings and track action items.

**Clarifying ownership and handoffs?** Use the [RACI Matrix](octoacme-raci-matrix.md) and [Cross-role Handoff Checklist](octoacme-handoff-checklist.md) to keep accountability and cross-role expectations explicit.
